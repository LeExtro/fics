  • EXT. FRONT OF UNIVERSITY - EARLY MORNING, WINTER
  • 1
  • The sun is barely beginning to rise with winter birds quietly chirping. Snow gently falls onto the ground. A banner hangs across the pillars of the front of the university; big, bright letters spell out "HAPPY HOLIDAYS (University Name)".

  • INT. DORMS, HALL
  • From the frosty windowsill, the sounds of birds echo slightly but are muffled by the walls. Focus slowly switches from the window to the falling snow and trees, frozen over by ice and caking in thick layers of snow.

  • WE slowly approach the door to HERO's dorm. The walls are sickly plain and pale. The weathered metal door, room number slightly askew, almost looming over us.

  • INT. DORMS, HERO'S ROOM
  • WE now see the room, looking down from the corner. Sunlight barely peeks through the shut blinds. The room is cramped and has a single bed, drawer and desk, all plain and well-worn. The walls are bright white and sterile, but look blue and cold with the dim sunlight. Notebooks, papers, pencils, pens and various other school items are scattered across the table, chaotically organized. A small tower of clothes pile against the side of the desk. Wrinkled covers and blankets lay atop HERO, slowly breathing.

  • WE then see the bed, looking straight down from the ceiling. A clock faintly TICKS. HERO curls in the fetal position. We faintly see his body rising and falling with his breathing.

  • And from the edge of the bed, WE see his face. His eyes are wide open with big dark spots underneath. A vignette closes in on his face. The TICKING of the clock intensifies slowly. The sound of TINNITUS slowly increases in loudness. The vignette fades away when the silence, TICKING and TINNITUS are broken by a loud ALARM CLOCK. HERO lies in bed for a moment, before turning over to slap the alarm clock quiet. He stays motionless for another moment before getting out of bed.

  • INT. DORM KITCHEN - MID MORNING
  • 2
  • WE see frosty window with snow caking the bottom of it. Snow continues to fall outside of the window, coating the trees. The winter sun fills the kitchen with a warm, calming glow and brighter colours (than his dorm). We see a pot of coffee on the boil from the side.

  • WE now look down at the stove top from the range, seeing breakfast sizzle in the pan. We slowly tilt down to see HERO's face looking slightly down while he cooks. A small smile appears on his face, before he looks up at us.

  • INT. LECTURE HALL
  • WE see the energy draining from HERO's eyes on his stone face as he listens on to the droning professor. The lecturer's voice starts muffled but slowly bleeds into an echoing clarity. HERO looks down to his notebooks and scribbles on, trying to catch up with the professor as they continue with the lecture, while we circle around him with quiet, slow, out-of-tune, eerie music in the background. We stop behind his shoulder.
  • PROFESSOR no. 1
  • (echoing from front of room)
  • ... {we will continue from yesterday with} -the musculoskeletal system. Page 587, chapter 17.6 of Human Physiology. You will note that te vertebral column consists of the cervical- {thoracic, lumbar and sacral vertebrae, etc. etc.}
  • The professor continues, but the words are progressively muffled. WE slowly look over his shoulder to see a textbook open, flipped to a page about the cervical spine. EAR RINGING gradually bleeds while we hear HERO's HEART POUNDING against his ribs, growing quicker and quicker with each passing second. We sweep over his shoulder, swinging around to see his face, trickling with sweat and paranoia. The THUMPING of his heart and the ringing rise to a crescendo before a male, UNNAMED FRIEND shakes him out of his panic attack.
  • UNNAMED FRIEND no. 1
  • (left of him, concern)
  • Hey, you alright there?
  • HERO
  • (unprepared, composing self)
  • I- uh... I'm fine, yeah. Just, zoned out for a second, that's all.
  • UNNAMED FRIEND no. 1
  • You know, you've been zoning out a lot recently-
  • HERO
  • (interrupting)
  • Sleep!... I mean uh- I haven't gotten the best sleep recently, honestly. You know... physiology being a pain and all with, all the latin words and what not.
  • WE watch HERO slowly get up from his seat, turning to scratch his head and shoot an awkward smile at his UNNAMED FRIEND which we can see over their shoulder.
  • UNNAMED FRIEND no. 1
  • The, test isn't even in a few weeks from now. I don't know why you're stressing so much about it.
  • HERO
  • (hiding behind a smile)
  • It's just-... again, the vocabulary is kinda iffy with me right now so, just trying to make sure I'm ready, right? Studying more doesn't hurt versus studying too little, right?
  • UNNAMED FRIEND no. 1
  • Guess so... don't kill yourself over it, though.
  • HERO
  • Ha, yeah, yeah...!
  • UNNAMED FRIEND no. 1
  • Anyway, we're going out for lunch again. Coming?
  • HERO takes a brief moment to think.
  • HERO
  • ...Sure! Yeah that, sounds good!
  • WE stay put and watch as HERO and his UNNAMED FRIEND walk out of the classroom, being the last ones out. The door shuts behind them.

  • EXT. TOWN STREETS - EARLY AFTERNOON
  • 3
  • WE see cars speeding by us from the curb. The town is not bustling enough to drown out the sounds of snow birds singing their songs. Snow continues to gently fall and add on to thick layers of snow on the sidewalk.
  • INT. MOVING CAR
  • WE see HERO's reflection in the front passenger window. HERO has his chin resting on a hand as he watches the cars drive by. His breath fogs up a small circle of the window. Conversation between his two male friends occurs around him, which he occasionally jumps into; he's still relatively absent-minded. We watch him reply to bits and pieces of the conversation, still over his shoulder, focusing on his reflection.
  • UNNAMED FRIEND no. 2
  • (left and back)
  • ... {You guys} -have any plans for Christmas? Actually surprised they're giving us some kind of break to begin with.
  • UNNAMED FRIEND no. 1
  • (left of, driving)
  • Probably gonna go back home, stay with family.
  • UNNAMED FRIEND no. 2
  • (jokingly)
  • Pfft, I came all the way out here just to leave family.
  • The three in the car, including HERO, laugh at the remark, though HERO doesn't laugh as much as the other two.
  • UNNAMED FRIEND no. 2
  • What about you, Hero? You got any plans?
  • HERO
  • (slightly unprepared)
  • Oh- uhh... probably the same thing, I guess. Going back to see family and what not. Mom's been worried sick, so.
  • UNNAMED FRIEND no. 2
  • Oh, yeah that's gotta suck. At least you don't have to introduce a new girlfriend or whatever, unlike smartass over here.
  • HERO winces slightly hearing the word 'girlfriend'.
  • UNNAMED FRIEND no. 1
  • Oh screw you too, damn virgin.
  • The two UNNAMED FRIENDS laugh again for a while. HERO stays a little quiet, but stone-faces through.
  • INT. CAFÉ
  • WE see the three of them sitting at a table by the window. Cars slowly drive by the window. The snow begins to fall harder. They are engaging in some small talk as we get closer to the table.
  • CUT TO: OVER SHOULDER, HERO
  • UNNAMED FRIEND no. 2
  • (complaining)
  • {...so} -yeah, English 2, living hell. Who would've thought [RAND. PROF. NAME] would be such a pain in the ass?
  • UNNAMED FRIEND no. 1
  • I'm just saying, you should've listened to the fourth years. They did tell us not to pick any language electives. Why would you even pick English as an elective if you're a STEM student?
  • UNNAMED FRIEND no. 2
  • Better than [RAND. M. STDT. NAME] and his stupid decision to pick art history.
  • HERO
  • Wait, art history?
  • UNNAMED FRIEND no. 2
  • I know. Brilliant decision making.
  • UNNAMED FRIEND no. 1
  • And I think someone else in our physiology class picked culinary, however the hell she managed it.
  • HERO is silent.
  • UNNAMED FRIEND no. 2
  • (after a moment)
  • ...That's actually not a terrible idea. At least she won't end up like you, eating cup ramen since day one.
  • HERO
  • (quietly, mumbling)
  • Yeah, that... doesn't seem like the most healthy decision.
  • UNNAMED FRIEND no. 1
  • (defensively) Hey! Cup Noodles are an important part of any nutritious breakfast- (cutting off) ...Oh, hey [RAND. F. STDT. NAME]! Didn't expect to see you here.
  • WE turn around with HERO to see a FEMALE STUDENT with a striking resemblance to someone he once knew. She is Asian and has long, straight, mid back-length, black hair. She wears an oversized, brownish beige, oversized, wool coat. She takes a seat next to him. We slowly pan off of his shoulder and onto hers. Everyone's voice starts becoming lightly muffled, with HERO's voice echoing clearly inside of him.
  • PAN BEGIN, SLOW & WIDE, SHLDR - SHLDR
  • FEMALE STUDENT
  • "Henry", is it? Heard a lot about you!
  • HERO
  • (a bit nervous)
  • Oh...? Why would, that be...?
  • FEMALE STUDENT
  • Well, seems like everyone knows you, huh? Even if they haven't seen you up close. Plus, you're involved in so much so, why wouldn't I?
  • UNNAMED FRIEND no. 1
  • Decided to invite her for lunch with us. Change things up a bit.
  • UNNAMED FRIEND no. 2
  • (smugly)
  • Wow, look at you. Already cheating?
  • UNNAMED FRIEND no. 1
  • (lightly annoyed)
  • Shut the fuck up man, jeez.
  • FEMALE STUDENT
  • (jokingly, sarcastic) Wow am I flattered. (regular tone) Anyway, yeah, he invited me here. I mean, I couldn't deny the offer. It's free food for one and, why would I refuse a chance to see the man himself?
  • HERO is blushing.
  • HERO
  • (flattered, slightly awkward)
  • I'll, take that as a compliment. ...Enough about, me. How about you...?
  • HERO idly listens on to the conversation between him, the FEMALE STUDENT and his two UNNAMED FRIENDS. He automatically replies to questions and adds in on to the conversation when relevant. The noises and talking around him get even more muffled. HERO's voice echoes louder inside of him.
  • PAN END INTO POV, F. STDT.
  • WE slowly zoom in on HERO's face.
  • ZOOM BEGIN
  • FEMALE STUDENT
  • {...I} -appreciate it. Well, I think I have to get going now. Talk to you later, Henry?
  • HERO
  • ...Sure, any time.
  • FEMALE STUDENT
  • Alright! See you later!
  • HERO
  • ...Yeah, you too.
  • UNNAMED FRIEND no. 2
  • (bit after, jokingly)
  • ...Dude, she's totally into you.
  • HERO
  • ...Maybe. Maybe she is.
  • ZOOM END, CLOSE-UP
